Theo Loo is a full-stack data scientist with 7+ years of experience spanning healthcare startups and the public sector. He received his B.S. in Microbiology from UW Madison and M.S. in Public Health from UC Berkeley.

Theo began his career researching waterborne disease transmission in South Africa and later managed the arboviral surveillance system for the State of Wisconsin where he contributed to the discovery of Borrelia mayonii, a novel bacteria that causes severe Lyme disease symptoms. In 2021, he became an epidemiologist at San Diego County, where he led COVID-19 outbreak surveillance and response efforts among schools. He also counsels UC San Diego Health on clinical outcomes research.

In addition to his public service roles, Theo owned health economics and outcomes research portfolios at several healthcare technology companies. He has authored numerous peer-reviewed articles in high-impact journals, including JAMA Network Open, evaluating clinical outcomes, quality, and return on investment. His work has directly contributed to securing 8-figure contracts, including the Children and Youth Behavioral Health Initiative, which provides free behavioral health services to children across California.

Outside of work, Theo enjoys surfing, scuba diving, and exploring new cultures through food while traveling.
